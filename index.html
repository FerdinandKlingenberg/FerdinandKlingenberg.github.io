Mathieu Laurière

Visiting Faculty Researcher

Google Brain, Brain Team



I am currently an assistant professor of Mathematics and Data Science at NYU Shanghai and a visiting faculty at Google research. I obtained my MS from University Paris 6 and ENS Cachan, and my PhD from University Paris 7. I was a Postdoctoral Fellow at the NYU-ECNU Institute of Mathematical Sciences at NYU Shanghai and a Postdoctoral Research Associate at Princeton University, in the Operations Research and Financial Engineering (ORFE) department. Prior to my current position NYU Shanghai, I was a Visiting Faculty Researcher at Google Brain, in the Brain Team (Paris).

My research interests include mean field control and mean field games, numerical methods, partial differential equations, stochastic analysis, machine learning, complexity theory and quantum computing. Here is my Google scholar page.

I am co-organizing a webinar on Machine Learning and Mean Field Games.
